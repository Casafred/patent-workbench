    <h2>Definitions</h2>
    <ul>
      <li itemprop="definitions" itemscope repeat>
        <span itemprop="subject">This document</span>
        <span itemprop="definition">relates, generally, to a ratcheting tool, and in particular, to a ratcheting tool having a torque limiting clutch.</span>
        <meta itemprop="num_attr" content="0001">
      </li>
      <li itemprop="definitions" itemscope repeat>
        <span itemprop="subject">a ratcheting tool</span>
        <span itemprop="definition">may include a head portion that can engage a work piece (for example, a fastener), and a handle portion extending from the head portion for manipulation by a user.</span>
        <meta itemprop="num_attr" content="0002">
      </li>
      <li itemprop="definitions" itemscope repeat>
        <span itemprop="subject">Rotation of the ratcheting tool in a first direction</span>
        <span itemprop="definition">i.e., rotation of the handle portion about the head portion</span>
        <meta itemprop="num_attr" content="0002">
      </li>
      <li itemprop="definitions" itemscope repeat>
        <span itemprop="subject">may cause a corresponding rotation of the work piece engaged with the head portion</span>
        <span itemprop="definition">for example, a tightening or a loosening of a fastener.</span>
        <meta itemprop="num_attr" content="0002">
      </li>
      <li itemprop="definitions" itemscope repeat>
        <span itemprop="subject">Rotation of the ratcheting tool in a second direction</span>
        <span itemprop="definition">may allow the handle portion of the ratcheting tool to be repositioned, while the head portion remains stationary, to provide for additional rotation in the first direction (and corresponding additional tightening or loosening of the fastener).</span>
        <meta itemprop="num_attr" content="0002">
      </li>

  <section itemprop="abstract" itemscope>
    <h2>Abstract</h2>
    
    <div itemprop="content" html><abstract mxw-id="PA739406211" lang="EN" source="national office" load-source="docdb">
    <div class="abstract">A ratcheting tool may include a motor coupled to an output drive mechanism by a clutch assembly. A torque selector may be rotatably coupled to a housing of the tool, to provide for selection of a maximum output torque. The clutch assembly may selectively disengage the motor and the output drive mechanism, or slip, in response to detection of an output torque level, or resistance torque, that is greater than or equal to the selected maximum output torque level.</div>
  </abstract>
  </div>

    <heading id="h-0001">FIELD</heading>
    <div id="p-0002" num="0001" class="description-paragraph">This document relates, generally, to a ratcheting tool, and in particular, to a ratcheting tool having a torque limiting clutch.</div>
    <heading id="h-0002">BACKGROUND</heading>
    <div id="p-0003" num="0002" class="description-paragraph">A ratcheting tool, or ratchet, may include a head portion that can engage a work piece (for example, a fastener), and a handle portion extending from the head portion for manipulation by a user. Rotation of the ratcheting tool in a first direction (i.e., rotation of the handle portion about the head portion), may cause a corresponding rotation of the work piece engaged with the head portion (for example, a tightening or a loosening of a fastener). Rotation of the ratcheting tool in a second direction (opposite the first direction) may allow the handle portion of the ratcheting tool to be repositioned, while the head portion remains stationary, to provide for additional rotation in the first direction (and corresponding additional tightening or loosening of the fastener). This ratcheting action may provide for relatively rapid tightening or loosening of a fastener while the tool remains engaged with the fastener. Some ratcheting tools may be relatively simple hand tools, while some ratcheting tools may be operated in both a power driven mode and a manual mode. Effective control of a maximum amount of torque output by the ratcheting tool and/or applied to the work piece/fastener may simplify use of the tool, may protect the work piece/fastener from damage, and may enhance the utility of the tool.</div>
    <div id="p-0004" num="0003" class="description-paragraph">Users face many problems or hinderances in the use of common powered ratchets. One problem is that a user may receive a reactionary force or kickback from the tool that can cause harm to the user. Another issue that users face in using powered ratchets is the necessity to use and/or purchase different tools or different ratchets to perform a job. Another problem users face is the necessity to purchase an entirely new ratchet or service the existing ratchet when a drive head is damaged. Therefore, a need exists for a ratchet tool with a torque limiting clutch.</div>
    <heading id="h-0003">SUMMARY</heading>
    <div id="p-0005" num="0004" class="description-paragraph">In one aspect, A ratchet may include a housing, a motor in the housing, an output drive mechanism coupled to the housing, a torque selector coupled to the housing, the torque selector including a collar rotatably coupled to the housing, wherein the collar is rotatable to a plurality of positions corresponding to a plurality of torque settings, so as to provide for selection of a maximum output torque of the ratchet, and a clutch assembly selectively coupling the motor and the output drive mechanism</div>
    <div id="p-0006" num="0005" class="description-paragraph">In another aspect, a ratchet tool may include a housing, a motor in the housing, an output drive mechanism coupled to the housing, and a clutch assembly selectively coupling the motor and the output drive mechanism. The clutch assembly may include a planetary gear set coupled to an output shaft of the motor, a ring gear having an inner circumferential surface configured to selectively engage the planetary gear set, a pin cage having a plurality of openings formed therein, a clutch washer, a plurality of clutch pins respectively received in the plurality of openings in the pin cage, each of the plurality of clutch pins having a first end thereof in contact with a first side of the clutch washer, and a second end thereof in contact with an axial end portion of the ring gear, a spring cage having a plurality of recesses formed in a first side thereof, a plurality of springs each having a first end thereof received in a respective recess of the plurality of recesses in the spring cage, and a second end thereof in contact with a second side of the clutch washer, and a pressing plate positioned proximate a second side of the spring cage.</div>
    <div id="p-0007" num="0006" class="description-paragraph">In another aspect, a ratchet tool with clutch may include a housing, a motor in the housing, n output drive mechanism coupled to the housing, a torque selector coupled to the housing, the torque selector including a collar rotatably coupled to the housing, wherein the collar is rotatable to a plurality of positions corresponding to a plurality of torque settings, so as to provide for selection of a maximum output torque of the ratchet tool, and a clutch assembly selectively coupling the motor and the output drive mechanism. The clutch assembly may include a planetary gear set coupled to an output shaft of the motor, a ring gear having an inner circumferential surface configured to selectively engage the planetary gear set, a pin cage having a plurality of openings formed therein, a clutch washer, a plurality of clutch pins respectively received in the plurality of openings in the pin cage, each of the plurality of clutch pins having a first end thereof in contact with a first side of the clutch washer, and a second end thereof in contact with an axial end portion of the ring gear, a spring cage having a plurality of recesses formed in a first side thereof, a plurality of springs each having a first end thereof received in a respective recess of the plurality of recesses in the spring cage, and a second end thereof in contact with a second side of the clutch washer, and a pressing plate positioned proximate a second side of the spring cage.</div>
    <div id="p-0008" num="0007" class="description-paragraph">In another aspect, a ratchet tool with limiting clutch may include a housing, a motor in the housing, an output drive mechanism coupled to the housing, and a clutch assembly selectively coupling the motor and the output drive mechanism. The clutch assembly may include a planetary gear set coupled to an output shaft of the motor, a ring gear having an inner circumferential surface configured to selectively engage the planetary gear set, a pin cage, a clutch washer, a clutch interface coupled to the pin cage, wherein the pin cage having a end thereof in contact with a first side of the clutch washer, and the clutch interface having an end in contact with an axial end portion of the ring gear, a spring cage having a plurality of recesses formed in a first side thereof, a plurality of springs each having a first end thereof received in a respective recess of the plurality of recesses in the spring cage, and a second end thereof in contact with a second side of the clutch washer, and a pressing plate positioned proximate a second side of the spring cage.</div>
    <div id="p-0009" num="0008" class="description-paragraph">This implementation of the invention, in particular, may be desired because it reduces the amount of reactionary force to a user and thus reduces the risk of harm to a user because the detection of maximum force can release the engagement of the motor and the output mechanism. This implementation of the invention may also be desired, in particular, because the coupling portion as configured to be removably coupled provides the user the ability to switch out one tool interface for another tool interface, thereby providing a user with options for different head assemblies and creating a plurality of different tools for many different jobs. Thus, to a relatively large extent, the interchangeable functionality of the drive assembly permits a user to forgo hauling multiple heavy tools and instead grants a user the ability to carry smaller tool heads for use with one main tool to perform different jobs.</div>

The exemplary tool <b>100</b> includes a motor <b>110</b> selectively engaged with an output mechanism <b>130</b> by a clutching mechanism <b>120</b>. The motor <b>110</b>, the clutching mechanism <b>120</b> and a portion of the output mechanism <b>130</b> may be received in and/or coupled to a housing <b>190</b>. In some implementations, the motor <b>110</b> may be an electric motor that receives power from, for example, a power storage device (such as, for example, a battery), an external electrical power source, and the like. In some implementations, the motor <b>110</b> may be an air driven, or pneumatic motor, that is powered by compressed air introduced into the housing <b>190</b> from an external compressed air source. The clutching mechanism <b>120</b> may include, for example, a slip clutch, that can selectively engage and disengage the motor <b>110</b> and the output mechanism <b>130</b>, based on, for example, a selected, or set, maximum output torque level for the output mechanism <b>130</b> for a particular application.</div>

    <div id="p-0032" num="0031" class="description-paragraph">A plurality of clutch pins <b>226</b> may be positioned between the clutch washer <b>224</b> and a corresponding axial end portion of a ring gear <b>227</b>. As the plurality of clutch pins <b>226</b> are axially movable in the openings in the pin cage <b>225</b>, compression and/or release of the compression of the plurality of clutch springs <b>223</b>, and axial movement of the clutch washer <b>224</b> in response to the compression/release of the clutch springs <b>223</b>, may cause corresponding axial movement of the clutch pins <b>226</b> positioned against the axial end portion of the ring gear <b>227</b>.</div>

Some embodiments of the clutch assembly <b>220</b> may include the pressing plate <b>221</b>, the spring cage <b>222</b>, the plurality of clutch springs <b>223</b>, the clutch washer <b>224</b>, the pin cage <b>225</b>, the plurality of clutch pins <b>226</b> and the ring gear <b>227</b> but may include fewer or more components to provide output torque control of the tool <b>200</b>.     <div id="p-0034" num="0033" class="description-paragraph"> <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>5</b> </figref> is a partial cross-sectional view of the exemplary ratcheting tool <b>200</b>, illustrating a connection of the motor <b>210</b> to the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, and a connection of the clutch assembly <b>220</b> to an input shaft <b>260</b>, or crank shaft <b>260</b>, for driving the output mechanism <b>230</b>, in accordance with implementations described herein. The principles to be described herein may be applied in power driven ratcheting tools including other types of motors, driven by other sources/types of power, for example the ratcheting tool <b>200</b> can be a cordless power driven ratcheting tool where the motor <b>210</b> supplied power by an interchangeable battery.</div>
    <div id="p-0035" num="0034" class="description-paragraph">In operating the exemplary power driven ratcheting tool <b>200</b> in the power driven mode, power may be transmitted from the motor <b>210</b> (via the motor shaft <b>212</b>) to the planetary gear set <b>228</b>, and on to the input shaft <b>260</b>, or crank shaft <b>260</b>. The crank shaft <b>260</b> may in turn transmit power to the output mechanism <b>230</b>, for operation of ratcheting components of the output mechanism <b>230</b>. In particular, in the power driven mode, power is transmitted from the motor <b>210</b> to the output mechanism <b>230</b> in this manner in a condition in which the ring gear <b>227</b> is rotationally locked, or essentially restricted from rotation. In a condition in which the ring gear <b>227</b> slips, or is allowed to rotate, power output by the motor <b>210</b> is not transmitted to the output mechanism <b>230</b>. That is, in the condition in which the ring gear <b>227</b> slips, the planetary gear set <b>228</b> may continue to rotate in response to power transmitted thereto from the motor <b>210</b>; however, due to the slippage, or rotation of the ring gear <b>227</b>, that power will no longer be transmitted through the crank shaft <b>260</b> to the ratcheting components of the output mechanism <b>230</b>. The torque level at which the ring gear <b>227</b> transitions from a fixed, or rotationally locked, or non-rotating state, to the slipped, or rotationally unlocked, or rotating state, may be controlled by the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, based on the maximum output torque level set, or selected through manipulation of the collar <b>270</b>.</div>
    <div id="p-0036" num="0035" class="description-paragraph"> <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>6</b>A</figref> is an exploded view of the pin cage <b>225</b>, the plurality of clutch pins <b>226</b>, and the ring gear <b>227</b>, and <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>6</b>B</figref> is a perspective view of the ring gear <b>227</b>. The exemplary implementation illustrated in <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>6</b>A</figref> includes three clutch pins <b>226</b> (<b>226</b>A, <b>226</b>B and <b>226</b>C) and three openings <b>225</b>A, <b>225</b>B and <b>225</b>C in the pin cage <b>225</b>, in which the clutch pins <b>226</b>A, <b>226</b>B and <b>226</b>C are respectively received. A number and/or an arrangement of the clutch pins <b>226</b> and the corresponding openings in the pin cage <b>225</b> may vary based on a particular combination of components. The clutch pins <b>226</b> are illustrated in this embodiments as pins but may be any As noted above, the clutch pins <b>226</b> may be axially movable in the openings in the pin cage <b>225</b>, based on a compressed/neutral state of the clutch springs <b>223</b> and a corresponding axial position of the clutch washer against the first end portion of the clutch pins <b>226</b>. In this implementation, the pin cage <b>225</b> is contained within the housing and allows an outer diameter ratchet head which allows the ratcheting tool to handle a higher load or torque.</div>
    <div id="p-0037" num="0036" class="description-paragraph">As noted above, the second end portions of the clutch pins <b>226</b> may be positioned adjacent to, or in contact with, the axial end portion <b>227</b>A of the ring gear <b>227</b>. The axial end portion <b>227</b>A of the ring gear <b>227</b> may be contoured, defining a ramped surface <b>229</b> including one or more ramps on the axial end portion <b>227</b>A of the ring gear <b>227</b>. In the example implementation illustrated in <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIGS. <b>6</b>A and <b>6</b>B</figref>, the ramped surface <b>229</b> includes ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B and <b>229</b>C (which may correspond, for example, to the clutch pins <b>226</b>A, <b>226</b>B and <b>226</b>C). A number, a contour, a placement, and the like of the ramps formed on the ramped surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b> may vary based on a particular combination of components.</div>
    <div id="p-0038" num="0037" class="description-paragraph">The second end portions of the clutch pins <b>226</b> may engage, or contact, the axial end portion <b>227</b>A of the ring gear <b>227</b>, moving, or sliding along the ramp surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b>. The clutch pins <b>226</b> and the ramped surface <b>229</b> are pressed against each other by a force applied by the clutch springs <b>223</b>. The magnitude of the force applied by the clutch springs <b>223</b>, causing the clutch pins <b>226</b> to move axially, may vary based on an amount of compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b>. As the output torque level increases, an output torque level greater than a set threshold (corresponding, for example, to the maximum output torque level set or selected through manipulation of the collar <b>270</b>) will cause the springs to compress, and cause the clutch pins <b>226</b> riding in the ramped surface <b>229</b> to jump, or ride over, the ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B, <b>229</b>C, causing the ring gear <b>227</b>, and the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, to slip or rotate. That is, as an amount of detected amount of output torque, and in particular, detected resistance torque, increases, the clutch pins <b>226</b> move along the ramped surface <b>229</b>, and up the ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B, <b>229</b>C. Due to the geometry of the ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B, <b>229</b>C, movement of the clutch pins <b>226</b> up the ramps in this manner causes the clutch pins <b>226</b> to move axially, toward the clutch washer <b>224</b>, thus moving the clutch washer <b>224</b> axially and compressing the clutch springs <b>223</b>. In response to detection of a torque level exceeding the maximum output torque level (set, for example through manipulation of the collar <b>270</b>), the clutch pins <b>226</b> will move, or travel, or jump over the ramps, disengaging the ring gear <b>227</b> and causing the ring gear <b>227</b>, and the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, to slip. In this slipped condition, the ring gear <b>227</b> will continue to rotate, or slip, while the trigger <b>296</b> is depressed and the motor <b>210</b> is generating power, but torque will not be transmitted to the crank shaft <b>232</b>.</div>
    <div id="p-0039" num="0038" class="description-paragraph">One type of clutch setting can limit the torque transmitted from the motor <b>210</b> to the output mechanism <b>230</b>. The amount of compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b>, affecting the positioning and movement of the clutch pins <b>226</b> along the ramped surface <b>229</b>, may vary based on an amount of output torque, and in particular, resistance torque, detected, affecting the axial position of the pressing plate <b>221</b>. The axial position of the pressing plate <b>221</b> may vary based on contact, or interface, or engagement of the pressing plate <b>221</b>, and in particular, the first and second arms <b>221</b>A and <b>221</b>B of the pressing plate <b>221</b>, with an interior geometry of the collar <b>270</b>. As the collar <b>270</b> is manipulated into a physical position corresponding to the selected maximum output torque level, this interaction, or engagement, between the pressing plate <b>221</b> (i.e. the first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B of the pressing plate <b>221</b>) and the interior geometry of the collar <b>270</b> may affect the torque level which causes the ring gear <b>227</b>, and the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, to slip or rotate. This interface, or interaction, will be described in more detail with respect to <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIGS. <b>7</b>A-<b>7</b>B</figref>.</div>
    <div id="p-0040" num="0039" class="description-paragraph">A second clutch type of setting may not limit the amount of the torque transmitted from the motor <b>210</b> to the output mechanism <b>230</b>. The clutch collar <b>270</b> has a setting where it positions the clamping plate <b>221</b> and spring cage <b>222</b> axially toward the clutch washer <b>224</b>, pins <b>226</b>, and ring gear <b>227</b>, in such a way as to prevent the pins <b>226</b> from axially moving out of the way of the ring gear ramp <b>229</b>B. The pins <b>226</b> are limited axially by the position of the spring cage <b>222</b>, and therefore prevent the ring gear <b>227</b> from slipping or rotating even though high torsional loading may be experienced at the output of the ratchet mechanism. In this implementation, based on the clutch setting, the power from the motor is not disengaged from the output mechanism and there may be a hard stop that prevents the ring gear from every spinning. It is a mechanical locking that prevents the ring gear from ever slipping.</div>
    <div id="p-0041" num="0040" class="description-paragraph">In some implementations, the torque level at which the ring gear <b>227</b> slips may be further affected by the coefficient of friction between the clutch pins <b>226</b> and the ramped surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b>, the contour, or angle of the ramps formed on the ramped surface <b>229</b>, the magnitude of the force applied to the clutch pins <b>226</b> by the compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b>, and other such factors.</div>
    <div id="p-0042" num="0041" class="description-paragraph"> <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>7</b>A</figref> is a side view of the collar <b>270</b> of the exemplary ratcheting tool <b>200</b>, and <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>7</b>B</figref> is a perspective view of the collar <b>270</b>, illustrating some of the interior geometry of the collar <b>270</b>. As described above, referring back to <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>4</b>B</figref>, the first arm <b>221</b>A of the pressing plate <b>221</b> extends through the first slot <b>295</b>A in the ratchet head housing <b>294</b>, and the second arm <b>221</b>B of the pressing plate <b>221</b> extends through the second slot <b>295</b>B in the ratchet head housing <b>294</b>. Distal end portions of the first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B may engage with a protruded portion <b>272</b>, or step portion <b>272</b>, of the interior of the collar <b>270</b>. The engagement of the first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B with the step portion <b>272</b> of the interior of the collar <b>270</b> may support an axial position of the pressing plate <b>221</b>, and/or set the axial position of the pressing plate <b>221</b>.</div>
    <div id="p-0043" num="0042" class="description-paragraph">As shown in <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>7</b>B</figref>, the step portion <b>272</b> of the interior of the collar <b>270</b> may include a contoured, or ramped surface <b>275</b>. An arrangement of the contouring, or ramping, of the ramped surface <b>275</b> along the inner circumferential portion of the collar <b>270</b> may correspond to a plurality of different maximum output torque level settings arranged along the outer circumferential portion of the collar <b>270</b>. The first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B of the pressing plate <b>221</b> may engage the ramped surface <b>275</b> of the step portion <b>272</b> of the collar <b>270</b>. In particular, the interface, or contact between, or engagement of, the first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B with a portion of the ramped surface <b>275</b> (based on the rotational position of the collar <b>270</b> and corresponding maximum output torque setting) will affect an axial position of the pressing plate <b>221</b>, based on the contouring of the ramped surface <b>275</b>. In some implementations, the first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B may be received in detents in the ramped surface <b>275</b> of the stepped portion <b>272</b> of the collar <b>270</b>, to provide a measure of feedback to the user when rotating the collar <b>270</b> to select a particular maximum output torque level. The axial position of the pressing plate <b>221</b> will in turn affect the amount of compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b>. The amount of compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b> will, as described above, affect the reactionary torque level causing the ring gear <b>227</b> to slips as described above.</div>
    <div id="p-0044" num="0043" class="description-paragraph">That is, as the collar <b>270</b> is rotated, the first and second arms <b>221</b>A, <b>221</b>B of the pressing plate <b>221</b> move along the ramped surface <b>275</b> on the inner circumferential portion of the collar <b>270</b>. The geometry, or contouring, or ramping of the ramped surface <b>275</b> causes the pressing plate <b>221</b> to move axially (for example, to the left or to the right in the example orientation shown in <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>4</b>B</figref>). For example, if the contouring of the ramped surface <b>275</b> were to cause the pressing plate <b>221</b> to move in a direction to compress (or further compress, or essentially fully compress) the clutch springs <b>223</b>, the additional force exerted on the clutch pins <b>226</b> may impede the movement of the clutch pins <b>226</b> up and over the ramps on the ramped surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b>. This may reflect, for example, a maximum setting for the allowable output torque level. Similarly, if the contouring of the ramped surface <b>275</b> were to cause the pressing plate <b>221</b> to move in a direction to release compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b> (for example, to the left, in the example orientation shown in <figref idrefs="DRAWINGS">FIG. <b>4</b>B</figref>), the lesser force exerted on the clutch pins <b>226</b> may impede the movement of the clutch pins <b>226</b> up and over the ramps on the ramped surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b> until the output torque level (detected resistance torque) reaches the maximum output torque level selected based on the rotational position of the collar <b>270</b>. In this situation, at the point at which the output torque level is greater than or equal to the selected maximum output torque level, the clutch pins <b>226</b> may ride up and over the ramps of the ramped surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b>, allowing the ring gear <b>227</b> to slip so that torque is not transmitted to the output mechanism <b>230</b>.</div>
    <div id="p-0045" num="0044" class="description-paragraph">As noted above, in some implementations, the exemplary power driven ratcheting tool <b>200</b> may be operated in a power driven mode, and in a manual mode (in which power is not transmitted from the motor <b>210</b> to the output mechanism <b>230</b> to implement a power ratcheting function). In some situations, a user may wish to operate the ratcheting tool in the manual mode, to allow for manual, or hand tightening of a work piece/fastener. This may also provide an advantage in protecting bits and fasteners from over stripping by the power driven ratcheting tool. In the exemplary power driven ratcheting tool <b>200</b>, in accordance with implementations described herein, the clutch assembly <b>220</b> is substantially entirely contained within the confines of the ratchet head housing <b>294</b>. The structural integrity of the ratchet head housing <b>294</b>, and containment of the clutch assembly <b>220</b> within the confines of the ratchet head housing <b>294</b>, may allow manual torque to be transferred, through the ratchet head housing <b>294</b>, to, for example, a square drive or square drive assembly of the output mechanism <b>230</b>, while still allowing the slip clutch assembly <b>220</b> to operate, and slip at a selected maximum output torque level as described above. This may allow the power driven ratcheting tool, in accordance with implementations described herein, to be effectively operated in the manual mode.</div>

The opposite axial end of clutch ramp profile <b>325</b> interfaces with a plurality of clutch springs <b>223</b> that press the clutch ramp profile <b>325</b> against the ramped surface <b>229</b> of the ring gear <b>227</b>. The magnitude of the force applied by the clutch springs <b>223</b>, pressing the clutch ramp profile <b>325</b> axially against the ramped surface <b>229</b>, may vary based on an amount of compression of the clutch springs <b>223</b>. As the output torque level increases, an output torque level exceeding the set maximum output torque level will cause the springs to compress, and cause the clutch ramp profile <b>325</b> riding in the ramped surface <b>229</b> to jump, or ride over, the ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B, <b>229</b>C, allowing the ring gear <b>227</b>, and the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, to slip. That is, as a detected amount of output torque, and in particular, detected resistance torque, increases, the ramped surface <b>225</b>A of clutch ramp profile <b>325</b> move along the ramped surface <b>229</b>, and up the ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B, <b>229</b>C. Due to the geometry of the ramps <b>229</b>A, <b>229</b>B, <b>229</b>C, movement of the clutch ramped surface <b>325</b>-<b>2</b>A up the ramps in this manner causes the clutch ramped profile to move axially, toward the spring cage <b>222</b>, thus compressing the clutch springs <b>223</b>. In response to detection of an output torque level that exceeds the set maximum output torque level, the clutch ramped surface <b>225</b>-<b>2</b>A will move, or travel, or jump over the ramps, disengaging the ring gear <b>227</b> and allowing the ring gear <b>227</b>, and the clutch assembly <b>220</b>, to slip. In this slipped condition, the ring gear <b>227</b> will continue to rotate, or slip, while the trigger <b>296</b> is depressed and the motor <b>210</b> is generating power, but torque will not be transmitted to the output shaft.</div>
